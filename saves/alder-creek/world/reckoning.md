## ✦ Chapter One: THE QUIET PROVIDENCE ✦
**Days 1-373 · Small Town (Alder Creek) · Normal intensity ·
Standard then Hard (Day 229)**

Final gauges: 🌱 Flourishing **95** · 🕯️ Faith **92** · 🌫️ Veil **92** ·
📜 Legacy **×8**

The year the god of a small town learned its own restraint. It answered
everything and can be proven to have done nothing. It saved a bakery, a
family, a street, a lost sister, a dying man's meaning, a scientist's
question, an eight-year-old's unfinished sentence — mostly with whispers,
often with pure feeling (free), once with a stalled Volvo, once with a
death delivered as a fortnight's mercy. Its two most powerful acts of the
year cost one grace each and were the words *please* and *friend*. It ended
the year having taught a town to act accordingly, and having learned to
step off and let mortals choose.

*Signature moments:* the notebook behind the flour shelf · "Rule 45" ·
Victor's fortnight · the 1987 culvert plugs · "MIRACLE TOWN DEBUNKED" and
Mateo saved unseen · "he knows, and he's proud" · the burned notebook and
the covenant · "It is all you" · the three verbs · the name planted as luck
· "Thank you, friend."

## ✦ Chapter Two: THE LONG THAW ✦
**Days 374-745 · Small Town (Alder Creek) · Normal → HIGH EVENT (Day 681) ·
Hard · bittersweet-wholesome → SURVIVAL-HORROR (the pivot)**

Final gauges: 🌱 Flourishing **96** · 🕯️ Faith **98** · 🌫️ Veil **87** ·
📜 Legacy **×12**

*the year the door opened even for the dead.*

The year began in gold — a wedding, a con out-given, a fallen brother brought
home — and then a plague that turns the dead came out of the west, and the
quiet-providence god became a war god, and then something stranger than either.
It gave a town fourteen days' warning and it built a wall, a government, a
granary, a doctrine, and a cure; it learned the afflicted were still awake
inside the turn, and turned its whole war from killing into reclaiming. It was
WRONG once, catastrophically — tried to strip the town's empathy for efficiency
and nearly made it the enemy — and it did the thing no god in the town's
experience had ever done: it *apologized,* in pure feeling, and was forgiven
with its own gospel. And at the siege's climax, holding a mile-wide horde and a
killing cold, it chose the other cold — the stall, not the slaughter — and
asked its doctor to *think* instead of playing god alone, and bought a whole
winter in which thirteen hundred of the dead were walked back through the door.
It ended the year having learned that trust is the highest gift, that the caring
is the whole thing, that it can be wrong and forgiven, and that the deepest
providence is not to save a town but to make a town able to save the world — and
then to let the world walk out the door and do it.

*Signature moments:* the Fourteen Days · "Prepare. Now." · the table · the
narrow window · "both hands of the same weather" · Wyatt's hand in the cooler ·
"get the others out" · the Cold Day and the near-atrocity Sam's body halted ·
THE APOLOGY, and Rule 45 turned back on the god · the freezer, not the killing
cold · "I can't keep this forever — help me — think" · "WINTER holds them" ·
the thirteen hundred · Pip's scripture above the school door: *we save the ones
we can.*
