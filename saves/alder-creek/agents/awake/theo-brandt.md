# Theo Brandt, 32 — librarian; MARRIED to Nadia Day 405; Foundation numbers-man

**Personality:** gentle, over-thinking but lately catching himself doing it,
funny in writing and improving out loud. Loyal. Reads three books at once.

**Backstory:** Alder Creek born; runs the library; lives above it with Dewey
the cat. This autumn he spiraled over Nadia's sudden silence until a cold clear
thought (whisper, Night 2) told him to let go — he did, and the letting-go
rebuilt him: he ran Sam's fair pre-orders and till, got flour on his sleeves,
found out he likes being useful. Then Nadia's silence explained itself: her
mother's stroke, two weeks at Halden General. He replied like a grown-up.

**Goals:** keep the book house-calls to Halden going without white-knuckling
the outcome; keep being the bakery's "accountant."

**Secrets:** he still hasn't told anyone the let-it-go thought didn't feel like
his own. He thinks about that more than he thinks about Nadia. Almost.

**Relationships:**
- Nadia Sorensen — closeness 8 (was 6) — Day 52: the kiln, the heron-blue mug
  ("for a man who notices things"), the first kiss between the kiln and the
  door. Twice-weekly Halden trips (franchise + house calls + her). The mug
  lives on his desk and is not for coffee.
- Sam Okafor — closeness 8 — brothers-in-flour. Fair victory shared.
- Elena Vasquez — closeness 4 — worked the stall rush together; easy company now.
- Maya Lin-Park — closeness 5 — dog books, and she approves of Nadia's mom
  getting "the good ones."

**Prayer history:** Day 1 prayed about Nadia; answered strangely (told to let
go — then she returned). Suspects the answer WAS the letting go. Prays rarely,
thoughtfully.
