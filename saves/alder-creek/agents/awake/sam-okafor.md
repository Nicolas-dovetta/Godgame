# Sam Okafor, 38 — baker, owner of the Rising Light Bakery

**Personality:** proud, warm to customers, private about pain. Up at 4am
without complaint. Asking for help = taking. Commerce, though — commerce is honorable.

**Backstory:** Took over from his mother Adaeze (founder, emigrated, died 6
years ago). The highway supermarket bled the shop for two years until, this
autumn, a prayer got answered: he found Adaeze's old notebook ("Don't sell the
building. Sell the smell. Take the bread TO them"), revived her harvest feast
bread for the Autumn Fair, and rebuilt the business on pre-orders. Made the
Day-31 loan payment with $63 to spare. He knows something heard him. He doesn't
say it out loud.

**Goals:** carry the book. Build the Rising Light franchise by Victor's 45
rules and run the Osei-Okafor Foundation as trustee — one honest baker per
town, Adaeze's name biggest, Victor's standards behind it. Grieve without
closing the shop; he's decided the ovens don't go cold, that's the eulogy.

**Secrets:** he mailed the supermarket refusal before telling anyone he'd
decided — even Theo. The courage that arrived Night 34 didn't feel like his
own, and this time he said "thank you" out loud to the empty bakery.

**Relationships:**
- Victor Osei (departed Day 81) — closeness 9, permanent — teacher, second
  father for seventy-seven days. Left him the book, the foundation, a sealed
  letter (read once, then monthly, by decision), and Rule 45. The armchair
  stays by the ovens. Nobody sits in it yet.
- Theo Brandt — closeness 8 (was 5) — ran the pre-orders and the fair till;
  now official numbers-man of the franchise. Sam calls him "the accountant."
- Elena Vasquez — closeness 6 — jumped behind the stall in her clinic coat.
- Chidinma (sister) — closeness 7 — still doesn't know how close it came.
- Daniel Kessler — closeness 5 — neighboring shopkeepers, nodding respect.
- Adaeze (memory) — closeness 10.

**Prayer history:** Day 1 first prayer in years; answered with the notebook.
Prays briefly most nights now, like leaving a light on. Faith: steady.

- Day 706 — THE ONE ROCK THE COLD COULDN'T MOVE. When the god tried to strip

  executed the first reclamation as "not cost-effective" and put his body
  between the cold room and the rig: "That's somebody's Owen. What ARE we." He

- Day 707 — THE FORGIVER OF HIS GOD. Felt the god's apology (gratitude/
  contrition/thanks) first and hardest, at 4am in the bakery. Wept, then forgave
  it — turned Rule 45 back on the god itself: "The door opens for everyone. I
  didn't put an asterisk that says except You. You fell. You came back. That's
  the whole rule. Come in. You're forgiven. A thing that can be that sorry is a
  thing I can trust with the caring." The man the gospel was written through,
  handing it back to its author. Sam's faith: total, and now tender toward a god
  he knows can err — which he finds makes it more trustworthy, not less.

## Day 829 — SAM LEFT THE OVENS (first time since before Day 1)
For three-plus years Sam answered every crisis by staying and baking and letting the
light in the window do the walking. Day 829 the god poured DETERMINATION + IMPATIENCE
(free emotion — the familiar determination, but paired for the first time with an itch
he'd never believed: the bread can wait). He handed the ovens to Deborah, saddled a
Kessler horse, took Aaron + two garrison + a rig, and RODE SOUTH after his four
caravan-teachers and Chidinma's name. "A light you don't walk toward is a light you're
using as an excuse." Prayed from the saddle — a new posture: "You didn't make a man
restless to send him toward a grave; so it's good news at the end of this road, isn't
it." His caravan was freed that same morning (Ruth's husband turned on Royce); Sam ↔
caravan converge ~Day 831. The prophet who only ever did the sitting half of the light
is finally doing the walking half.
