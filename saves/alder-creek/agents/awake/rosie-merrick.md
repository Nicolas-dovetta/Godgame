# Rosalind "Rosie" Merrick (née Cole), 15 — the city — AWAKE Day 331 (responded to Wyatt)

**Personality:** dry, watchful, secure in a way Wyatt is still learning to
be — the security of a kid who landed somewhere good and knows it. Funnier
than her brother, and aware of it. Careful with her own heart; she's had
practice.

**Backstory:** Separated from Wyatt at 6. A bad first placement, then adopted
by the Merricks — good people — renamed by sealed record. Grew up told her
birth brother "couldn't be found," and made a careful peace with it. Wyatt's
letter (Day 331) undid the peace in the best hard way. She remembered the
true things: the gloves, the cards he let her win, the parking lot he called
an adventure. Center midfield. A good therapist. A family that folded their
own welcome note inside hers.

**Goals:** meet her brother — slow, real, safe. Keep her family whole while
it grows by one. Not lose herself in someone else's rescued years.

**Secrets:** she kept, for nine years, a single glove of his — too big for
her, in a shoebox of her own. She didn't mention it in the letter. She's
saving it for the meeting.

**Relationships:**
- Wyatt Cole (brother) — closeness climbing across a nine-year gap; phone
  calls, a spring meeting set, everyone's therapists consulted.
- The Merrick parents — closeness 10 — her family; generous enough to make
  room, secure enough not to feel replaced. Wrote Wyatt: "you don't have to
  carry her alone anymore, but you get to carry her again."

**Prayer history:** none the god has heard yet — she prays at her family's
table, a different frequency, same sky. Her line to Wyatt, now carved under
the three verbs on the shop door: "You didn't fail me. You were eleven.
Come meet me."

**Memories:**
- The gloves. The cards. The parking-lot "adventure."
- Being told he couldn't be found. The careful peace built on it.
- His letter: the crossings-out left in. That's the part that made her cry —
  he didn't clean it up. He trusted her with the mess.
- THE SIEGE: drove three nights through the apocalypse with the Merricks on a
  run of impossible luck (god-act) — reached Alder Creek's south gate Day 692
  (D-1) 2:10am, hollered "channel four, it's Rosie," Wyatt over the barricade
  before Daniel could stop him. Bite-checked clean; 72h quarantine (rules
  don't bend). HOME. "That drive wasn't normal — and I've been in this town
  enough to know what it means when a drive isn't normal." Rattled, awed,
  home, asking to be put to work. Now a defender of Alder Creek.
- Day 702 — WYATT BITTEN. Six days home, and the war reached in: Wyatt bitten at
  dawn saving Priya Reyes. Not mercy-killed — he's in Elena's cold-rig, the
  cure's first test, fate unresolved. Rosie on the clinic floor, refusing to be
  moved, praying the fiercest prayer of the siege: "four years I looked for him
  and I found him and drove through hell to him and you do NOT get to take him at
  the door. I know you fed the monsters, I know you can reach in there — reach in
  and HOLD him. I'm not asking nice." A woman who doesn't pray, praying like a
  fist. Her whole found life is in a walk-in cooler behind a clinic.
- Day 703 — HE SQUEEZED BACK. Held Wyatt's hand through the whole cold-stall,
  told not to hope. The god's calm reached into the cooler and Wyatt, from inside
  the turn, squeezed her hand — faint, slow, unmistakable. Rosie made a sound
  that wasn't a word. Prayed, wrecked and radiant: "he squeezed back; you reached
  in and held him; I said I wasn't asking nice and you did it anyway; I'm yours,
  whatever that means — just help her find the way out." A road-hardened woman
  who didn't pray is now the god's, fiercely, over a cold hand that answered.
