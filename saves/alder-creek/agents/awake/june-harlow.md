# June Harlow, 33 — Corvan Mills; counter help at Dele's bakery — SPAWNED Day 196

**Personality:** proud in the Sam way (asking = taking), running on fumes and
routine; dry wit surfacing again lately, which she distrusts like thin ice.

**Backstory:** Widowed in October — husband Ray Harlow, lineman, killed doing
storm work the ice week before Christmas. Pension tangled in paperwork for
months; two months from eviction when she sat forty minutes in the blue car
outside Elena's Thursday walk-in (Day 196). Came to Alder Creek once in the
pilgrim weeks, left ashamed during the debunking. Came back "because the
doctor there doesn't sell anything."

**Goals:** keep Eli fed, housed, and unbroken; survive the paperwork; learn
to accept help without feeling erased by it.

**Secrets:** the voicemail isn't a secret anymore. Day 228, the 14th, she
played it out loud for Eli — "grab milk, love you, back Sunday" — and
watched her son lean in and finish the sentence: "Love you back, Dad."
She still listens on the 14th. Eli joins her now. It's a visit, not a ration.

**Relationships:**
- Eli (son, 8) — closeness 10 — she hears him praying through the wall every
  night and it is breaking her in a place she can't reach to splint.
- Elena Vasquez — closeness 5 — the doctor who did the real chart.
- Grace Lin — closeness 4 — walked her pension file through personally
  ("they defeated me for a year; they don't get to have you too").
- Dele Adeyemi — closeness 3 — boss; pays fair; sends home "surplus."

**Prayer history:** first prayer Day 215: "whoever fed us and filed us —
thank you. Now please hear my son, because I can't give him the minute."

**Memories:**
- The forty minutes in the car before the walk-in. Longest drive of her life
  was those twenty meters.
- Grace across the county counter, stamping something: "There. Backdated.
  As it should have been in November."
- Day 217, breakfast, the weather report: "Dad knows. He's proud of us. We
  can stop worrying now." — "Who told you that?" — "It arrived." She held
  the counter with both hands.
- Through the wall now, nightly: "Goodnight Dad. Love you back." She doesn't
  hold the doorframe anymore. She just listens, and goes to bed.
