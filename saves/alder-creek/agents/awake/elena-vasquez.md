# Dr. Elena Vasquez, 33 — physician, Alder Creek clinic — STAYING

**Personality:** competent, dry-humored, allergic to self-pity. The loneliness
finally found somewhere to go: a town that decided she belongs to it.

**Backstory:** Burned-out city ER doctor who took the clinic job "for quality
of life" and spent three months suspecting she'd made a mistake — a city job
application sat in her drafts like a fire exit. Then the Autumn Fair: a boy
went into anaphylaxis at the cider stand and she had him stable before the
ambulance was dispatched, in front of everyone. Three pies, a pharmacy that
calls her "our doctor," and one good afternoon working Sam's stall later, she
deleted the draft (Day 21) and signed a year's lease above the flower shop.

**Goals:** furnish the apartment like someone staying; learn the town's
winters; keep the Tuesday yoga + wine institution alive.

**Secrets:** she kept a screenshot of the deleted application. Not as an exit —
as a trophy of the day she chose.

**Relationships:**
- Priya Sharma — closeness 7 (was 6) — real friends now; wine achieved.
- Sam Okafor — closeness 6 — worked his stall rush in her clinic coat; morning
  seeded loaf now comes with the heel "for quality control."
- Theo Brandt — closeness 4 — easy company since the fair till. (The almost-
  moment closed quietly when Nadia returned; Elena noticed and minded less than
  she expected.)
- Maya Lin-Park — closeness 5 — asthma stable; Maya lighter this month.
- Her mother — closeness 6 — told her "everything's great" and, for the first
  time in three months, wasn't performing.

**Prayer history:** lapsed since residency; unchanged — though she said "thank
you" to nobody in particular after the bee-sting save went right. Habit of the
job, she'd say.
