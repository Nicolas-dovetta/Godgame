# Marek, ~40 — ex-Royce rifleman turned Halesford door-keeper — SPAWNED Day 836
# (name given Day 836; "Ruth's husband," "the wavering man" in earlier entries)

The crack in the wolf's wall. Rode with Royce's crew for a month, swallowing his
anger, while his wife **Ruth** lay cold-kept in a Halesford co-op cold-room three days
south — and never knew there was a door until Alder Creek's teachers came up Royce's
road. The town's offer of bread (which Royce refused) broke something open in him:
*"you don't say no to bread unless you've decided people are things."*

**Day 829 — THE TURN.** The god poured pure ANGER into him (free emotion, no words) —
the overdue fury he'd been swallowing. He put his rifle to Royce's ribs in the coldland
barn where the caravan was held, said Ruth's name out loud, and tipped the barn in 90
seconds (two other waverers followed); no shot fired, the caravan walked free with him
and a coldlands route he'd helped hold. **Day 836 — RUTH RECLAIMED.** He learned the
cure with his own hands at Halesford and watched his wife wake up furious and alive; sat
on the cold-room floor and wept. Now the keeper of the 5th daughter door.

**Personality:** quiet, not a speech-man; a slow fuse and a straight aim, both now
pointed at doors instead of gates. Carries the specific moral clarity of a man who stood
on the wrong side and got shown the other one without being shot for it.

**Goals:** be a door for the rest of his life; teach Halesford and the coast; and —
his own mission — find every angry man on every warlord's road and teach him "the fury
feels better spent on the cold-room than the gate. Send them to me. I know that road. I
used to guard it." A recruiter of the converted, aimed back at Royce's own kind.

**Relationships:**
- Ruth — wife, reclaimed Day 836 — the reason for the anger, the turn, all of it. Awake
  soon; recovering.
- Alder Creek / Sam — closeness 6 fast — the town that taught him without punishing him;
  Sam detoured a caravan to reach Ruth's cellar and he will not forget it.
- Royce — former captain, now repudiated; Marek is the living proof of why Royce is
  losing (you can't garrison the rain with the thirsty).

**Prayer history:** two unwitting prayers (Day 828 at the table; Day 829 in the barn) +
Day 836 on the cold-room floor — doesn't know the god's name; suspects the anger that
saved Ruth "came from outside me... maybe the good ones never do [feel like yours]."

**Memories:**
- The bread Royce wouldn't take. The anger that arrived like a fever breaking right.
- The rifle against Royce's ribs; Ruth's name said out loud; the barn tipping.
- Ruth waking up mad about her hair and alive; weeping on the cold-room floor.
- The vow: teach the angry men that fury belongs at the cold-room, not the gate.
