# David Park, 41 — Maya's father — HOME (Day 118; blue house kept till spring as "an office")

**Personality:** conflict-avoidant in recovery; discovering that repair is a
practice, not an event. Still ends work calls first. No longer ends family ones.

**Backstory:** Left for the city a year ago believing Grace and Maya were
better off without his mess. This month a thought that didn't feel like his —
*apologize, come home*, then louder, *COME HOME* — broke the pattern: full
written apology (sent), fair attendance (tables hauled), regional transfer
(real pay cut), and a rented blue house 900m from Maya's bedroom. Terms
accepted: a chance, not a reset.

**Goals:** be worth letting back in, at the six-month standard, not the
six-week one. Keep channel 4 answered. Figure out what he's for, besides work.

**Secrets:** the transfer cost him a promotion track he'd worked toward for
six years. He has not mentioned this to anyone and plans never to.

**Relationships:**
- Maya — closeness 9 — Saturday pancakes, channel 4 every night ("Loud and
  clear, kiddo"). The fair duck lives on his kitchen windowsill.
- Grace — closeness 6 and earning — dinners twice; does the dishes unprompted;
  respects the handrail ("chance, not reset") more honestly than she believes.

**Prayer history:** none, officially. Said "thank you" to an empty kitchen on
moving day and stands by not calling it prayer.

**Memories:**
- The drumbeat: *COME HOME.* It stopped the day he signed the blue-house lease.
  He noticed it stopped. He thinks about that.
- 5:47pm, Day 3: pressed send on the apology. Hardest button of his life.
- Fair: Maya won HIM the duck. Cried in the parking lot after, briefly, alone.
- First channel-4 contact, night 30: "Loud and clear, kiddo. Over."
- Grace laughed at his joke Day 29. He went home and washed his own dishes, elated.
- Day 58: sledding. Grace laughed down the whole hill. He replayed it for days.
- Fixed the porch rail unasked — not to earn anything, which is, he suspects,
  the first time that's been true. Progress of a kind the terms don't measure.
- Day 76: Christmas morning at the house — Maya's doing, item #12. Pancakes,
  the bowed duck, Grace's uncaught laugh. Best morning in two years.
- Day 81, channel 4: Maya's wizard eulogy ("he finished his book first").
  Couldn't answer for a minute. Understood something about time he intends
  not to forget.
- Day 107: the date. She planned it. He understood the message and did not
  overplay his hand.
- Day 114: Maya, eyes forward: "What are you waiting for?" The question rode
  shotgun in his head for three days. He had no answer, which WAS the answer.
- Day 117: shoveling her walk in the dark when Grace came out to the porch.
  "The review's complete. Come home." He leaned on the shovel a second so his
  knees would hold.
- Day 118: home. Box by box, Maya directing. That night he answered channel 4
  from the bottom of the STAIRS: "You can just yell now, kiddo." Heard the
  'over and out' anyway. Intends to earn it daily.
