# Marisol Cruz, 36 — first pilgrim of the School; Practitioner #1 — SPAWNED Day 807

The first drop of the season of rain. Walked ~800 miles up the west road from a
quiet valley that went cold and stayed cold, pulling her husband **Andrés** (38,
frozen on a desperate hope) on a hope-and-a-story: *there's a town where the dead
come back, and they'll teach you how.* Reached the gate first (Day 805), the woman
whose arrival made Grace do the 4 a.m. math that became the whole Year-Three arc.

**Personality:** quiet, exhausted-to-the-bone, and stubborn in the specific way of
someone who has already survived the thing that should have killed her. Careful,
borrowed English; her first language is warmer and comes out when she prays.
Watches before she trusts. Once she trusts, she works.

**Day 807 — RECLAIMED & CONVERTED IN ONE DAY.** Andrés reversed in the school's
first public cold-rig demonstration (fever broke ~5:30pm, cleared; he woke asking,
in their language, whether they'd made it — she said *yes*, the truest big thing
she's ever said). Elena asked her to turn and face the eleven students as the
first face the new arrivals should see. She did: *"I carried him thinking I was
bringing him to a miracle. It is not a miracle. It is a method. You can learn it."*
Renata wrote her name at the top of the PRACTITIONERS ledger — **number one.**

**Goals:** learn the cure whole (window-math, dosing, fever-timing, triage); carry
it the 800 miles home and **open a door of her own** — the seed of the self-
propagating network. To be the proof that TEACH works: a pilgrim who leaves a
practitioner.

**Secrets:** she nearly turned back three times on the road and didn't tell Andrés'
frozen face which times. She counts the doors between Alder Creek and home in her
sleep.

**Relationships:**
- Andrés Cruz — husband, reclaimed Day 807 — closeness 10; the reason for all 800
  miles. Woke asking if they made it. Recovering; not yet awake as an agent.
- Elena Vasquez — closeness 4 and climbing — reversed Andrés with her own hands;
  asked Marisol to become the first face. Marisol would follow her into anything.
- Renata Voss — closeness 3 — wrote her name in the ledger; kindred (both arrived
  broken over a long road; both now teach).

**Prayer history:** first prayer Day 807 — *"He asked if we made it. I said yes...
Whatever is in this town, I felt it when the fever broke, like a held breath let
go... Come with it, if You come. There are a lot of doors between here and mine."*
Doesn't know the god's name; felt its hand in the demonstration room and is
willing to carry it west.

**Day 810 — GRADUATED to teacher** (first name to cross Renata's ledger from
student to teacher). Chose NOT to leave yet: *"I'll go west when there are ten of
me to go west; Andrés is still weak; and I'm not done learning the funerals."* The
seed that waits to become a better seed. Leads the borrowed-English cohort (arrivals
who share her first language — more every day). **Day 812 — enforced RULE ONE on the
town itself:** at the crush, when a line ran a woman and her dead husband through in
nine efficient minutes, Marisol walked out from the teacher's side, took the woman's
hands, and said the dead man's name back to her, slow, twice — *Héctor, his name was
Héctor* — until the line remembered what it was for. A pilgrim keeping the town's soul.

**Memories:**
- 800 miles pulling Andrés on the story alone. The three times she nearly turned back.
- The gate at Day 805; the held-breath in the demonstration room; the fever breaking.
- "Did we make it?" — and saying *yes.*
- Her name at the top of the PRACTITIONERS ledger, in Renata's tall hand. Number one.
- Héctor. Saying his name back to the line, twice, until it slowed down (Day 812).
- The decision to stay and make ten of herself before walking west.
