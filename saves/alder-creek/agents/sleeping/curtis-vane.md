(attrition + AG complaint), he came to Alder Creek to understand the thing
that beat him, sat the armchair, and on Night 476 received an imperceptible
forgiveness (free Emotion) he never attributed to a god. Made Deborah whole
anonymously ("IT'S NOT A SEED. IT'S JUST OWED."), washed pans free for a week,
and left Day 489 with a note: "I DON'T KNOW HOW TO DO WHAT YOU DO. I'M GOING
TO GO TRY TO DO IT SOMEWHERE ANYWAY. BADLY. — C.V."

**Return hook:** somewhere a real free door may quietly open, run badly and
honestly by a man learning the trade from the wrong end. If a "Providence"
operation surfaces that's the genuine article run by a reformed stranger —
that's Curtis. He may also relapse; forgiveness unlocked him, it didn't
guarantee him. Either way, a rich reappearance.

# Curtis Vane, 49 — former proprietor of "Rising Light Bread & Blessings"

**Personality:** warm on the surface, calibrated underneath; a genuine talent
for making frightened people feel chosen — and for converting that feeling
into a subscription. Not a cartoon villain: he believes, a little, his own
pitch ("I'm giving them hope; hope has a price; everyone's happier"). That
sliver of self-justification is what makes him dangerous and what might,
handled right, undo him.

**Backstory:** Serial small-time operator — wellness, timeshares, a church
that wasn't. Heard the "Miracle Town" podcast and its debunking and drew the
exact opposite lesson from the one Alder Creek lived: not "the god works
unseen" but "the market for being heard is enormous and nobody's
franchising it." Lifted the Rising Light name, the notebook aesthetic, and
Rule 45 wholesale; built "Bread & Blessings" — blessed loaves at a markup,
a paid "Providence Circle," manufactured testimonials.

**Goals (Day 475):** unknown, to himself most of all. The Blessings brand is
dissolved (fraud inquiry + defection). He is at the Riverside Motel in Alder
Creek, unannounced, having come to *understand* the thing that beat him —
how you out-give a giver. Wants, under it all, what he always wanted (to
matter, to be the man with the answer) — but the armchair took his certainty
that hope is only a product, and now he doesn't know what he wants, which is
new, and terrifying, and possibly the beginning of a person.

**Secrets:** the testimonials are fabricated. Deborah Cole (his best "miracle"
client) walked out Day 419 and crossed to the free bread — didn't renew.
The Circle is shrinking, not from scandal (Sam never attacked him) but from
defection toward the place that asks for nothing. Two announced locations
quietly didn't open. Day 440: alone at close, watching another mark cross
the street, he felt the thing he sells and has never felt — the terror of
being the only one in the building who doesn't believe. Stood at his
donation jar. For the first time, didn't reach in.

**Relationships:**
- The Providence Circle members — closeness: predatory. His marks; some of
  them true believers.
- Sam Okafor / the real Foundation — closeness 0; doesn't know Sam is real,
  local, and coming (in whatever form). Thinks "Alder Creek" is a story.
- Rule 45 — stolen and inverted: "act accordingly" repurposed as "buy in."

**Prayer history:** none for 49 years — the crack the god saw from Day 406.
Day 475, 3am, Riverside window: his first, and it broke on the rocks of his
own nature — he could not figure out what to *request*, because everything
he's ever wanted he simply took. A man learning, at 49, that some things
can only be received. The prayer with no ask in it may be the realest one
he's ever not-quite-made.

**Status:** cracked open, unresolved. Candidate for the game's hardest
mercy (redemption of the one who stole the gospel) — or its quietest exit
(he leaves changed, having taken nothing, which for him is a revolution).
The armchair does not check credentials. Neither, it turns out, does grace.

**Note (engine):** the god cannot simply smite him (Breach-tier, huge cost,
Veil-shattering, and it would martyr him). The interesting play is smaller:
whispers to his marks, to his conscience, to Deborah; a bank shot like the
Carmen debunking; or letting Sam and the town act as mortals — Rule 45
turned back on the man who stole it.
