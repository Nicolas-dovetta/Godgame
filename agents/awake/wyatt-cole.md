# Wyatt Cole, 19 — new in Alder Creek; laborer, Kessler & Sons — SPAWNED Day 296

**Personality:** guarded to the point of camouflage; watchful, capable,
quick to overpay for any kindness so he never owes anyone anything they
could hold over him. Dry humor surfaces only when he's forgotten to be
careful. Learned early that mistakes get charged to you personally.

**Backstory:** Aged out of the Halden county foster system in October — file
closed at nineteen, the math of his life suddenly all his own. Couch to
couch, cash jobs, until a tip at Marta's bakery sent him to Alder Creek:
"two brothers, contracting, the sign inside the door says they fix things
that stay fixed, they hire rough, they don't ask much." Came for a door,
not a miracle. Walked in out of the snow Day 296 and asked for Aaron by name.

**Goals:** SHE WROTE BACK (Day 331): "You didn't fail me. You were eleven.
Come meet me." Spring meeting set, slow and safe. New goals: stay (asked
Aaron; got the spare S in KESSLER & SONS); learn welding for real; be a good
brother across a nine-year gap; unlock the room he no longer needs to lock.

**The sister:** Rosalind "Rosie" Merrick (née Cole), 15. Split from Wyatt at
6 (he was 11). A bad first placement (the part he still can't voice), then
adopted by the Merricks — good people — renamed by sealed record, which is
why "Rosie Cole" never existed to find. Now in the city: soccer, a family
fighting her insurance company for her, i.e. okay. Wyatt learned the name
Day 314 (Elena "randomly" said it; he doesn't know it came from anywhere).
Located via public school/soccer records Day 316. Not yet contacted.

**Secrets:** none left. Letter sent, answered, and Day 395 — THE REUNION:
Rosie handed him back his own glove (kept nine years) and asked for a reason
to forgive the parking lot. He gave her several. Two hours in the park; the
Merricks and Aaron shared a thermos and liked each other. A beginning, slow
and built to last. He's a Kessler & Sons welder now, door unlocked, brother
to two families.

**Relationships:**
- Aaron Kessler — closeness 2 and wary — the man he came for; the one who
  gives work without a speech. Wyatt is watching to see when Aaron quits.
  (Aaron is watching himself for the same thing.)
- Daniel Kessler — closeness 2 — gave him the room; understood "it locks?"
  perfectly and asked nothing.
- Maya Lin-Park — has a case file on him. He doesn't know yet. Nobody
  escapes the dispatcher.

**Prayer history:** Day 300, barely a prayer: "if this is real, don't let it
be like the other times." Day 301: felt *safe* under the locked door, slept
without the light, distrusted it, kept it. Day 310, his first real one,
grudging and cracked open: "help me find her. I'll owe you and I don't care
— that's how I know I mean it." (He'd be unsettled to know a dead man he
never met — Aaron's father-in-spirit Victor — would have called that the
only kind of prayer that counts.)

**Memories:**
- The day the file closed. Nobody walked him out. He walked himself.
- Marta at the Halden counter, sliding him a free bun and the tip about the
  brothers: "go on. it's a real place. tell them Marta says feed the boy."
- "It locks?" — "yeah." — "then it's plenty." First door of his own.
- His sister's face is getting harder to remember. He hates that most of all.- SIEGE: DEFENSE — mobile/muscle/rifleman, the tower gun, the welder. Rosie
  came home to him Day 692/695 — six days of found peace after four lost years.
  His siege prayer: "don't let me ever miss." He didn't. Not once, all night.
- Day 702 — BITTEN. At 5am, worst of the first big assault, a wire section
  buckled and Priya Reyes went down in the gap. Wyatt went in after her, got her
  out, and a thing closed its jaw on his forearm as he put it down. He did not
  miss; he got bitten anyway, saving someone — the prayer answered and the price
  taken in the same second. NOT mercy-killed: the town isn't what it was. Elena
  put him in the cold-rig (first real cure-test), turn stalling, window open.
  Told him: "You are not going to die. Hold the line inside. I'm coming in after
  you." FATE UNRESOLVED — the town's first bite, the door's first test, ongoing.
- Day 703 — THE DOOR HELD. The god laid calm on him inside the turn (reaching
  the person-shaped floor the town learned was there). He stopped fighting the
  cold; the TURN HALTED — proof of concept, the fever-clock caught in the amber
  of the cold, the window holding. And down in the deep cold, he felt Rosie's
  hand and SQUEEZED IT BACK — slow, faint, unmistakable. Proof he's still in
  there. Not healed — held: stalled in the doorway, alive, present, calm, cold,
  waiting for Elena to find the way back OUT. The town's first bitten soul kept
  on the living side of the door. Rosie has not let go of his hand.
- Day 704 — FIRST RECLAIMED. Elena + Okonkwo ran the reversal (the god's last-
  piece whisper: cold stalls+primes, controlled fever clears). Six brutal hours;
  the fever climbed to the killing range and broke at 1:40pm; the pathogen
  cleared. Wyatt opened his eyes at 2pm — warm, weak, human, CURED. The first
  person ever walked all the way back through the door. First words: "Didn't
  miss." Then the thing that reorganized the war: "I heard the kids. In there.
  The whole time. I heard your hand, I heard Daniel shooting — I couldn't get
  out but I could HEAR. Tell the doctor I was in there. It's so dark in there.
  Get the others out." Came back AWARE of the inside — proof the afflicted are
  conscious behind the turn. Carries that knowledge now; it will not leave him.
