# Nadia Sorensen, 30 — potter, Halden — ENGAGED to Theo, Day 210

**Personality:** warm, direct, hands-first (pottery, climbing, gardens); allergic
to games. Went silent on Theo not from cooling but from a life falling on her:
her mother's stroke, two weeks sleeping in a hospital chair.

**Backstory:** Grew up in Halden; studio behind her house — the famous kiln.
Met Theo at the county book fair; two weeks of daily texting (bouldering,
birdwatching, herons); then the stroke and the silence. Resurfaced Day 13.
Her mother is home now, walking with a cane, reading Theo's house-call books.

**Goals:** her mother's recovery; the winter kiln firing schedule; and Theo —
whom she has decided about, in the way she decides about glazes: completely.

**Secrets:** the "coincidence" of being at Iversen's Bakery on Day 42 took her
forty minutes of deciding what to wear. She'd deny the number under oath.

**Relationships:**
- Theo Brandt — closeness 7 and climbing — Day 52: showed him the kiln, gave
  him the heron-blue mug ("for a man who notices things"), kissed him between
  the kiln and the door. He takes the bus. She finds this endearing past reason.
- Her mother, Ingrid — closeness 9 — recovering; sharp-tongued; already asked
  when "the library boy" is coming back.
- Marta Iversen — closeness 5 — childhood neighbor; Nadia does the bakery's
  new Rising Light license sign lettering (small towns braid everything).

**Prayer history:** none in years; said "thank you, whoever's on shift" to the
hospital ceiling the night her mother squeezed her hand again.

**Memories:**
- Theo's messages during the silence: kind, unpushy, then — nothing demanded. Rare.
- Day 42, Iversen's: he looked up from the royalty ledger and lost his place in
  the sentence. Logged. Treasured.
- Day 52: the kiln, the mug, the kiss. He held the mug like a relic all evening.
- Day 210: the bird guide, third table, the ring in the cut compartment, the
  BACKUP COPY (she will tease him about this at their fiftieth anniversary).
  Said "OBVIOUSLY" loud enough for three stalls. Wedding next spring, the
  library. She's throwing the tableware herself: heron-blue, obviously.
- The commission: heron-blue mugs for every Rising Light bakery — her kiln is
  the franchise's official cup. She fires them in batches of forty and signs
  the bottoms with a tiny heron.
- Drove down the day Victor died, found Theo in the stacks. Held him. Decided
  (again, harder) about him. Her mother's bridal magazines are HER doing too;
  she lets Ingrid take the blame.
