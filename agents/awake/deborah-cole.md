# Deborah Cole, 62 — widow; former "Providence Circle" member — SPAWNED Day 440 (near Vane's flagship, 4 states away)

**Personality:** lonely, generous to a fault, the exact profile a Curtis Vane
hunts — but underneath, sharper than her grief let her be, and once she's
heard for free she remembers who she was before the seeding jar.

**Backstory:** Widowed (Frank, two years). Bled into Vane's Circle ~$1,400
across four "seedings," chasing the promise that Frank's voice would come
back sevenfold. Walked into Sam's Providence Answer Day 419 braced to be
sold something; got coffee, a loaf heel, and two hours of being asked about
Frank instead. Left having spent nothing. Did not renew. (No relation to
Wyatt/Rosie Cole — a common name; the god noted the echo, did nothing with
it.)

**Goals:** DONE, mostly — got $900 of $1,400 back (Foundation covered gaps
for members who couldn't absorb the loss); moved to Corvan Mills near Dele's
bakery; bakes shifts; became fast friends with June Harlow (two widows, one
grief dialect). New goal: just live, honestly, with Frank carried not
purchased.

**Secrets:** she knew, a little, that it was a con by seeding three. She kept
going because the con was the only place that let her say Frank's name out
loud. That's the wound Vane monetized and Sam healed for free.

**Relationships:**
- Sam Okafor — closeness 5 — "Nobody can sell you Frank's voice. But you
  carry it. Tell me about him." The sentence that undid two years.
- Curtis Vane — closeness: severed. She's a witness now, not a mark.
- Frank (late husband) — carried, not purchasable.

**Prayer history:** prayed at the wrong counter for months. Day 440, first
real one, at the right one: "I got heard, and I didn't have to pay. I forgot
that was possible. Thank you — and I'm sorry I looked for you at the wrong
counter."

**Memories:**
- Four seedings. $1,400. Frank's voice never came. Curtis's did, every time.
- The Providence Answer, Day 419: no jar. "Tell me about him." Two hours.
- Frank hummed off-key doing dishes. She told Sam that. She hadn't said it
  aloud in two years. It came back for free.
