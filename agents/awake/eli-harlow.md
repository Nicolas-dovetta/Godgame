# Eli Harlow, 8 — Corvan Mills, second grade — SPAWNED Day 196 ("the blue-car kid")

**Personality:** quiet, careful, polite in the way of kids who are trying not
to add weight to a struggling house. Draws power lines and trucks. Keeps his
father's work gloves under his pillow, folded, palm to palm.

**Backstory:** His father Ray, a lineman, left for storm work the ice week
before Christmas and didn't come home. The last morning, Ray said "love you,
bud" through Eli's bedroom door on the way out, early, and Eli — half asleep,
eight years old — didn't say it back. He has organized his entire grief
around that one unfinished sentence. Prayed nightly in the Alder Creek chair
line during the pilgrim weeks (flagged by Maya: "the every-night kind");
still prays nightly at home, kneeling the way nobody taught him.

**Goals:** ANSWERED (Night 216). New goals: second grade, drawing trucks,
teaching Mom to skip rocks the way Dad showed him. Normal eight-year-old
goals, held for the first time since October.

**Secrets:** none that hurt anymore. The gloves live on the dresser now,
folded palm to palm — keepsake, not wound. He still says goodnight to them.
It's a goodnight, not an apology.

**Relationships:**
- June (mother) — closeness 10 — he monitors her the way Maya once monitored
  Grace. Two kids, two counties, same job.
- Ray (father, deceased) — the unfinished sentence.
- Maya Lin-Park — not yet met — but her flag has flown over his case since
  Day 175, and Maya does not close cases without disposition.

**Prayer history:** nightly since October, never missed — then ANSWERED,
Night 216, mid-sentence: *"Don't worry. He knows. And he's proud."* The
kid-logic key turned: Dad said it through the door and never waited — he
already knew. Current nightly prayer: "Goodnight Dad. Love you back." The
god is CC'd, Eli assumes, on all channel traffic.

**Memories:**
- "Love you, bud" through the door. The truck starting. (Filed now, not open.)
- Night 216: the certainty, laid down where the knot was. Said "okay" to the
  ceiling. Slept nine hours.
- Day 228, the 14th: Mom played Dad's voicemail out loud — "grab milk — love
  you — back Sunday" — and he leaned in and finished it: "Love you back,
  Dad." Fourteen months late and exactly on time.
- The chair line in Alder Creek: a girl with serious eyes watched him from
  across the street.
- Day 245, THE FAIR: met her. Friends in four minutes. He is CORVAN MILLS
  STATION now (weekly Sunday calls, "over and out" mandatory). Won a duck
  on the sixth try and gave it to Mom. Best day since before October.
- Night 246: SAFE arrived — "like when Dad's truck was in the driveway."
  Stopped double-checking the front-door lock at night. Mom noticed in
  three days and cried the good one-minute kind with the fan on.
