# Aaron Kessler, 43 — contractor; staying at the Riverside Motel (for now), re-roofing Kessler's Hardware

**Personality:** charming at a distance, guarded up close — but the guard has
been standing down in shifts. Keeps score; discovered this month the score was
kept wrong.

**Backstory:** Younger Kessler son. Gerhard was hard on him; Daniel was the son
who stayed. Left at 19, built a contracting business two states away, came back
only for the funeral and detonated at the wake. Returned this autumn, divorced
since April, with a drafted lawyer's letter to force a sale of his half of the
store. Then: the fair nod; the closing-time fight; the shoebox — 24 birthday
cards, returned unopened by his father, kept for years by his brother. The
score was kept wrong. He burned the letter in the motel sink (Day 19) and
offered the roof instead: $12k of his own labor as a buy-in. Staying through
the job. Possibly longer. Hasn't said so.

**Goals:** be, for Wyatt Cole, the adult nobody was for young Aaron — without
knowing how, and terrified of quitting the way everyone quit on the kid
before. "Fix things that stay fixed" is painted inside the door; a person is
not a west-wing.

**Secrets:** he suspected Victor's fortnight from the will and the armchair
and said nothing — kept a dying man's pace without being asked. His letter
marked AARON said: "You knew. Thank you for letting me spend it my way. Fix
things that stay fixed. — V.O." He keeps it in the truck's glovebox and has
read it at four red lights.

**Relationships:**
- Daniel Kessler — closeness 5 and climbing (was 2) — brother again,
  provisionally. The cards rewrote 24 years of "nobody wanted me here."
- Jonah Reyes — closeness 2 — Kettle regular now; Jonah comps his coffee.
- Sam Okafor — closeness 2 — buys feast bread Fridays; introduced himself
  properly; Sam remembered his mother liked Aaron. News to Aaron. Good news.

**Prayer history:** none. "Asking the ceiling for a refund." Though on the
night of the shoebox he sat in the motel parking lot for an hour, and if
gratitude counts, the ceiling got some.

**Memories:**

- Day 127: augered the Halden bend. "Ice layered like bad plywood."

- Day 137: the crest hit his channel and the jam tore loose in truck-sized

- West-wing drywall lost; "drywall was always a coward." Rebuilding.
- Day 140: prayed at the '72 with Daniel, in unison, unplanned. Second prayer

- Day 296: Wyatt Cole walked in out of the snow the way Aaron once did.
- Night 301: the answer — "Love, educate, advocate for. That is all you need
  to do. I believe in you." Nailed the three verbs to a scrap of lumber
  inside the door, under "fix things that stay fixed." Stopped trying to be
  a father in some huge unpicturable way; just did the three verbs, badly
  then better.
- Day 310: the advocate verb cracked the boy open — a lost sister, four
  years of looking. Days 314-345: the name surfaced (a lucky doctor), the
  letter went, Rosie wrote back ("come meet me"), spring meeting set. Wyatt
  asked to stay; Aaron pointed at the sign — "sons, plural, we've had the
  spare S the whole time" — adoption by signage, the Kessler way. Aaron is
  a father now. Did it with three verbs and did not do it like Gerhard.
- SIEGE: barricade & THE LINE engineering (the wall, the siding kill-lane, the
  dropped bridge span). Steel and geometry against the end of the world — the
  Kessler verbs turned to survival.
- Day 698 — FIRST BLOOD. Took a gash to the forearm at the siding wire during
  the first wave, a turned's hand through before Wyatt dropped it. NOT a bite —
  ninety seconds of the whole net holding its breath, then Elena's word: CLEAN.
  Stitched, benched, furious about the benching (= fine). From the cot: "a
  hand's width. tell Wyatt I said good shot. tell the kid I'm sorry I scared
  him. and — fine — thanks for the hand's width." The town's first wound of the
  hot siege; a hand's width from its first bite.
