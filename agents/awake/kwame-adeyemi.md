# Kwame Adeyemi, 24 — Dele's younger brother; Corvan Mills (returned Day 631) — AWAKE

**Arc:** the resolved thread of "The First Believer." Vanished in March after
his failed business left Dele liable for a co-signed loan — fleeing not the
law but his brother, sure staying gone was a gift. Located Day 597; got
Dele's letter; snagged on "I don't know how to be forgiven." Night 621 the
god's whisper unsnagged him — "you don't need to know how; it isn't your
decision; the only thing you can do is ask." Called Dele Day 623; came home
Day 631; handed an apron, fed without interrogation, staying. A repair in
progress; going to be fine.

**Personality:** warm, impulsive, a dreamer who bets big and lands hard;
buried under two years of shame into something quiet and small. Genuinely
did not know Dele stole to cover him — thought the debt just crushed them
both and that his absence at least stopped it from also being awkward.

**Goals (unresolved, his):** figure out whether going back helps Dele or just
forces everyone to look at him; learn the thing he says he never learned —
how to be forgiven.

**Secrets:** he almost called Dele eleven times. He keeps Dele's letter in
his apron (the brothers share a gene: aprons, guilt, honesty deferred).

**Relationships:**
- Dele Adeyemi (brother) — closeness deep and wounded; the letter ("you were
  never the curse; you were my brother; still are") is doing its slow work.
- The Foundation / Alder Creek — closeness 0, but the letter described a place
  that catches people, and Kwame, who believes he is uncatchable, can't stop
  rereading the part about being fed.

**Prayer history:** none since boyhood until Night 621's whisper unlocked it.
Day 645, first real one, learning the grammar: "I asked. That's all I had and
it turned out to be all I needed. I still don't know how forgiveness works
and you told me I don't have to and I've decided to believe you."

**Memories:**
- The business: his idea, his pitch, Dele's signature under his. It felt like
  hope. It was a hole.
- The last text he sent Dele: "sorting it, don't worry, love you." The lie
  he's been living inside ever since.
- The letter, Day 603. The line he can't get past: "The only hole left is
  where you're supposed to be."
