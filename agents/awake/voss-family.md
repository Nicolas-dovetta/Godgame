# The Voss family — refugees taken in Day 694 (first contact) — SPAWNED Day 694

The family that brought the siege to the wire. Fled the western outbreak
eleven hundred miles. Their son turned at the gate; the surviving three were
bite-checked clean and taken into quarantine — the door still opening for the
living, even now.

## Cal Voss, 44 — diesel mechanic
Drove his family across a dying continent. Broke at the wire begging the town
to believe his bitten son might be immune (he wasn't). Watched Owen turn and
be put down by the doctrine. Grief like a collapsed building. Grace has him on
the defense roster for when quarantine ends — "a town that survives gives the
grieving a wall to build." A skilled mechanic: engines, generators, the
water-tower pumps — genuinely valuable to a besieged town. Guilt: he knew
about the bite in Nebraska and drove five more days pretending.

**Day 696 — BROKE USEFUL.** Out of quarantine, he walked straight into the
defense briefing and asked for a job before anyone offered one: the grain-siding
genset for the reception op, and keeping the water-tower line from freezing.
Elena had predicted he'd "break useful or break bad depending on whether
somebody got to him before the work did" — the god poured determination on the
whole town that night (free emotion, no words to Cal specifically), and the
guilty man reached for the nearest useful thing. His prayer — *"let me be worth
the door they opened; I don't want forgiveness, I want to be useful"* — is being
answered by his own hands, which is the only way it ever could be. Live thread:
the guilt isn't gone, it's channeled; grief deferred into work can still come
due later.

## Renata Voss, 41 — schoolteacher (teaching again)
Could not speak for a day. Elena sat with her an hour and did not try to fix
it. **Day 700 — CAME BACK via the work:** walked into the school-on-the-hill
and started teaching the 11 children inside the wall, because a teacher who has
lost her son knows better than anyone that routine is a shelter you build
indoors. Still goes quiet mid-sentence, still flinches at the tower gun — but
she teaches. Pip's in the front row ("Voss station, in school, over"). Prayed
her first prayer, chalk on her hands: "I taught today. Owen would've sat in the
back pretending not to listen. Thank you for the eleven who did." The town's
2nd teacher; proof people don't just survive a siege, they live inside it.

## Pip Voss, 7 — youngest
Watched her brother die an hour before the gate. Given a walkie-talkie by
Maya, who designated her "Voss station — you report to me; we don't lose
people to silence; over." Held the radio like a hand and said "over." Maya has,
without discussing it, adopted Pip into the network and the family of the
found. The two children may be each other's way through this.

## Owen Voss, 16 — DIED / turned Day 694 (the siege's first death)
Bitten six days before the gate; hid it; turned at 6:02am twenty feet from
safety. Never savable — the boy was gone before the wagon arrived. The town's
first mercy-kill of the new world, decided by the table together, executed by
Daniel. Carried by everyone, owned alone by no one. (No separate departed file;
recorded here and in the chronicle as the first name on the siege's wall.)

**Note (engine):** the Vosses are the human face of the siege's cost and the
proof the town kept its soul — the door still opened. Cal (mechanic) and
Renata (teacher) are real assets; Pip + Maya is an emotional throughline.
Cal's survivor-guilt is a live thread.
