# Dele Adeyemi, 27 — baker, Corvan Mills; Foundation's FIRST licensee — SPAWNED Day 555 (recurring since Day 92)

**Personality:** earnest, gifted, proud in the exact Okafor key (asking for
help = taking); the true believer of the Foundation, which is precisely why
his fall is a tragedy and not a crime story. Bakes like it's a sacrament.
Currently: a good man six months into a bad thing, unslept, hollowed.

**Backstory:** Drove two hours with a cooler of samples to the Foundation's
first-ever interview (Day 92); cried in the walk-in when he signed. Built
Corvan Mills into a thriving licensed shop; anchors the town's node with June
Harlow and Deborah Cole. Then March: his younger brother **Kwame** — whom
Dele co-signed a business loan for two years back — vanished owing everything,
leaving Dele personally liable for a debt that will take his shop, his house,
and his mother's care-home fees. Too ashamed to invoke the Foundation (built
for exactly this), he began "borrowing" from the royalty remittances he
controls — meaning to repay, keeping a repayment spreadsheet like a rosary.
~$9,000 short over five months. Falsified the books well enough to fool
anyone but Theo, who reads them like scripture.

**Goals (Day 575):** repay the $9k (now a formal Foundation loan, on the
books, in daylight); be the door for the next person who falls; find Kwame
alive. CONFESSED Day 557 — of his own will, before he knew he'd been caught.
Re-tethered to a god he thought had hung up.

**Secrets:** none. Repayment ahead of schedule; back on the till co-signed
(Day 615), trust visibly re-earning; teaches the Armchair School session
"What To Do Before You Do What I Did" (most-attended ever). No open wounds:
Kwame CAME HOME (Day 631) — called first (Day 623), Dele finished his
"I don't know how—" with "you don't have to; just come; there's a chair."
Handed him an apron at the bus stop. Both brothers home, honest, mending;
ledger square by spring. Dele: "I'll be the door forever."

**Relationships:**
- Sam Okafor — closeness 7, about to be tested — his mentor, the man whose
  own Day-1 sin (pride, not asking) Dele is unknowingly re-enacting worse.
- Theo Brandt — closeness 5 — the bookkeeper who found it; carrying it in
  agony.
- June Harlow & Deborah Cole — closeness 5 — feed him, worried; don't know why.
- Kwame Adeyemi (brother) — vanished; the loan; the wound.

**Prayer history:** a real nightly pray-er; believed the theft severed the
line. Night 556: the whisper — "Come out clean. It is never too late." — plus
strength/courage, proved the line was never cut. Confessed at dawn Day 557.
Now prays re-tethered, daily, the gratitude kind: "you never hung up."

**Memories:**
- The cooler of samples. Crying in the walk-in. The proudest day of his life.
- Kwame's last text: "sorting it, don't worry, love you." Then nothing. Then
  the collection letters in Dele's name.
- Rule 46 on his shop wall — "It is all you" — which he now cannot look at.
- The repayment spreadsheet. Column H is how much he still owes. Column H
  does not go down fast enough. Column H is his whole prayer life now.

**Note (engine):** this is the game's oldest theme — pride, not asking —
worn as a crime. The hard fork: justice (ruin + the death of Rule 9's
premise) vs. cover-up (corrupt the thing to save it) vs. the third path
(the town does for Dele, on the far side of a theft, what he was too ashamed
to let it do for a hardship — mercy that still reckons with the wrong).
